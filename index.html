The Problem — Five Failure Layers

Science is not the bottleneck.
Friction is.

Most deep tech failures are attributed to bad technology, wrong market, or inexperienced founders. This is the wrong diagnosis. After 29 years operating across 60+ countries, the pattern is clear: the technology usually works. The failure is structural — the commercial adoption system was never engineered to eliminate friction.

The Innovation Friction framework treats commercial adoption as a physics problem. Every failure has a layer. Every layer has a mechanism. Every mechanism has a solution. The five failure layers must be eliminated in sequence.

The Four Denominators

What sits below the line
determines everything.

The numerator — technology and execution — is what founders obsess over. The denominator is where companies die. Each variable has a specific, engineerable reduction mechanism.

μhuman
Human Resistance
Psychology, loss aversion, career risk, cultural inertia. The internal friction every buyer carries before any product demo.
Reduced by: Reference customers · risk-sharing pricing · Three Truths narrative
Nlegacy
Legacy Normal Force
Incumbent systems, vendor contracts, regulatory certifications, sunk infrastructure. The structural weight your technology must displace.
Reduced by: Beachhead segmentation · greenfield entry · compatibility bridges
τcycle
Temporal Friction
Sales cycle × approval layers. Every month consumed is capital burned. τ and C_capital are directly coupled — the most dangerous denominator interaction.
Reduced by: Pilot-first models · pre-committed LOIs · procurement mapping
Ccapital
Capital Friction
Total capital required to reach cash-flow positive. When C_required ÷ C_available approaches 1.0, the company fails regardless of all other variables.
Reduced by: Path A or C selection · CRL 5 as primary milestone · revenue-based financing
